Rules for Entering Dates

 Last Revised: 02/11/06

Excellware strives to design software that enables the computer to help you do your job.  We also want to prevent data entry errors whenever possible.  This is especially true when entering dates.

The following rules apply to all date entries:

  1. You do not need to enter the / characters.  You can if you really want to, but its usually easier just to enter the numbers.
  2. You do not need to enter the year.  Dynamo will append a year depending on the month you entered, and the current month.  Dynamo will choose the closest year.  For example, in December, or even August, if you enter 0101, Dynamo will assume January 1 of next year.  Similarly, in January or even June, if you enter 1231, Dynamo will assume December 31 of last year.  Try leaving the year off when entering dates.  Dynamo will guess correctly most of the time.
  3. You do not need to enter the month unless it is different from the current month.  So you can simply enter 10 and in July 1995, the date displayed will be 07/10/95.
  4. The leading zero is not required for the month nor for the day.  For example, you could enter 85 and in 1995, the date displayed will be 08/05/95.  But in cases of ambiguity, such as 111 the program adds a leading zero to the month and assumes 01/11 not 11/01.
  5. The letter T can be used whenever you want today's date.  Once you enter a T, you can still use option 6 below.
  6. Touch + or - to advance to the next or previous date.  Note that the = character also acts like + so you don't have to hold the shift key down.
  7. Touch W or w to advance to the next or previous week.
  8. Touch C or c to bring up calendar for selecting date.
  9. Year 2000 issues: This program does not permit the operator to enter the complete 4 digit year.  Rather, it assumes the century based on today's date.  It allows date entries in the past 90 years, the current year, and the next 9 years. For example, if today is 2/14/99 and you enter a year of 00, 01, 02, 03, 04, 05, 06, 07, or 08, then the year will be assumed to be 20xx.  If you enter 09, 10, 11,...,99 then the year will assumed to be 19xx. If you need to enter a date that is earlier than 90 years ago, or more than 9 years from today, please contact your software supplier or Excellware, Inc.
  10. If you've made some entries and want to return to the original default date, simply touch the Escape key.  Note that there is sometimes a brief pause after touching the Escape key before the original date is displayed.
