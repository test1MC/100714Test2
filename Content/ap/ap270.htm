Form 1099 Processing

 Last Revised: 01/10/14

A 1099 Form is a government form that an employer is required to provide to individuals who are not employees but who are paid more than $600/year.  

These forms can be printed anytime; for example, forms for the calendar year 2015 can be printed in February 2014.

Form 1099 Processing offers the following 5 options:

  1. Print Summary Report of all vendors that are either currently coded to require 1099 forms or have one or more invoices that are coded for 1099s.   The "other" columns on this report indicate other invoices to this vendor that are not coded for 1099s. Sample summary report.
  2. Print Detail Invoice Report to list the specific invoices not coded for 1099s.   You can use the A/P Invoice Maintenance program on the A/P menu to modify the 1099 form status for any invoice. Sample detail report.
  3. Summarize 1099 Data by Vendor.
  4. Maintain 1099 Data by entering a vendor and 1099 type.   Descriptions of the 1099 boxes display by using the F2 key.
  5. Print 1099 Forms for all vendors or an individual vendor.  Only laser forms are supported. Currently there is a separate form for each 1099 type (Dividend, Interest, or Miscellaneous). There is a Federal copy, Recipient Copy, and Payer Copy for each type.  Excellware can provide the compatible forms. Sample 1099 form.

Constant information, such as your address, telephone number, and federal taxpayer ID number that is printed on 1099s, can be maintained using A/P Parameter Maintenance on the APU menu.
