PRO/Tracking# Maintenance

 Last Revised: 02/10/16

Use this program to edit the tracking number of a particular order. If you have a scanner, you can scan the bar code on the billing copy of a shipment to get the number. Otherwise, enter the number listed below the bar code. The rest of the information will automatically fill in, but you can edit it if necessary. After completing or updating the information in this application, the PRO/Tracking# will be displayed when viewing this sales order in Sales Order Inquiry.

Pro/Tracking Maintenance Primary View
Bar Code# Scan the bar code from the notification of shipment/billing copy or manually enter the number that is printed below it.
Order Type The type of order, such as Sales Order, Transfer Order, or Purchase Order.
Order# The Dynamo order number associated with the order being viewed.
Ship To Name The name of the individual or company receiving the order. If there is an "Attn" (attention) line, use Address Line 1 for this and then enter the street address on Address Line 2.
Ship to Address, City, State/Country, Zip The address of the individual or company receiving the order. When entering the zip code, there may be multiple cities to choose from. Choose the one that is most appropriate. For most carriers, the zip code carries more weight than the actual city name when routing a package to its destination.
Date Shipped Enter the date on which the order was given to the shipping carrier or the label was created.
Carrier Enter the carrier that is delivering the shipment, such as UPS, FedEx, or the United States Postal Service. Press the F2 hotkey for a quick list to select from.
Service Indicate the level of service through which the carrier will deliver the shipment. Examples are Ground, Next Day Air, etc.
Carrier to Bill This indicates who the shipping carrier will send an invoice for the shipping charges. Most often, companies that are shipping the order [(S) Shipper] will also pay the carrier for the shipping cost and pass those costs to the customer in some way (e.g. by adding shipping charges to their order). Less often, but still feasible, a company will have the shipping charges billed to the customer's account with the shipper [(R) Recipient]. For third party billing (T,) see below for details.
Carrier to Bill Acct# Most companies that ship regularly already have an account number with their preferred shipping carrier(s) which allows for volume shipping discounts as well as use of the carrier's shipping software. Some customers, however, may wish to have the shipping charges billed to their own account for various reasons.
Third Party Customer A third party is someone other than the shipper or receiver. An example might be a corporate headquarters that pays for certain types of shipments to its franchise locations. If a third party customer is being billed for this order, enter their account number here. Be sure that the account number is with the carrier that is delivering the package (defined in the Carrier field above).
Weight The final weight of the package can be obtained internally via a scale or externally from the carrier once they have weighed the package.

 

If specified in your Company Wide Parameters (CWP menu), you can print the Bill of Lading after approving all of the information. This can be given to the shipper to indicate the contents of your shipment.
